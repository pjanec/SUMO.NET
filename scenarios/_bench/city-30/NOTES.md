Generated by `scripts/gen-benchmark.sh 30`. See `provenance.txt` for exact commands/seed/hashes.

## Engine capability gap found (netgenerate feature avoided)

The network is generated at **`-L 1`** (one lane per edge), not the multi-lane (`-L 2+`)
BENCHMARK_SPEC.md asks for. Multi-lane grids exposed a real engine gap, reproduced directly (not
guessed):

- `netgenerate -L 2 --tls.guess` on the same 3x3 grid + `randomTrips.py --fringe-factor 5` demand
  reliably makes `Sim.Core.Engine.LoadScenario`/`TryInsertOnLane` throw
  `InvalidDataException: No <connection> found from edge '...' lane N to edge '...'` for a small
  fraction of multi-edge routes.
- Root cause: `NetworkModel.ResolveLaneSequence`/`ComputeBestLanes` and
  `Engine.TryStrategicLaneChange` (C2-ii) are explicitly scoped, per their own header comments in
  `src/Sim.Core/Engine.cs`, to a **single-look-ahead** route-to-lane resolution (the first edge
  transition only) plus a **same-edge** drop-lane convergence at runtime. There is no full
  multi-hop strategic replan: if the greedy lane walk for edge transitions 2+ in a route lands on
  a lane with no `<connection>` to the next required edge, insertion fails outright.
- Per `CLAUDE.md`/the benchmark briefing, this was **not** worked around by touching `Sim.Core` —
  it is scoped out at the generation layer instead: `-L 1` makes every lane's out-connections
  unambiguous (exactly one connection per reachable direction), so route-to-lane resolution can
  never be ambiguous, at the cost of not exercising lane-changing/overtaking at this rung.
- **Follow-up (real finding, not benchmark-specific):** any future rung that wants `-L 2+` to
  exercise lane-changing needs `ComputeBestLanes`/`ResolveLaneSequence` extended to a genuine
  multi-hop lookahead (or the lane sequence re-resolved dynamically at each junction instead of
  once at insertion). Until then, scaled-city rungs stay single-lane.

## Demand-format gaps closed (not engine bugs, just generation-side adaptation)

- `randomTrips.py -r` / duarouter's *default* output embeds `<route edges="..."/>` as a child of
  `<vehicle>`. `Sim.Ingest.DemandParser` only supports the two-part form (`<route id=.../>` +
  `<vehicle route="id"/>`). Fixed by routing with `duarouter --named-routes`, which emits exactly
  that form.
- Neither `randomTrips.py` nor `duarouter` emit a `<vType>` by default, so vehicles carry no
  `type=` attribute. SUMO defaults such a vehicle to its own built-in `DEFAULT_VEHTYPE`; the
  engine's `DemandParser`/`Engine.LoadScenario` does not synthesize that default and throws
  `KeyNotFoundException` on `VTypesById[""]`. Fixed by a post-process step
  (`gen-benchmark.sh add_default_vtype`) that inserts an explicit
  `<vType id="DEFAULT_VEHTYPE" vClass="passenger" sigma="0"/>` and references it from every
  `<vehicle>`.
